14 September 2018

Sushil Pandit

Talk on
28 years after the Exodus of Kashmiri Pandits

Sushil Pandit is a Kashmiri Hindu and co-founder of Roots-In-Kashmir (RIK), which works with Kashmiri Hindus who grew up in exile. Among other issues, RIK fights for justice to the victims and survivors of the Hindu Genocide in Kashmir while preparing the next generation of leaders for their struggle.
Sushil ji publishes Praznath, a bilingual journal on the culture and identity of Kashmir. He is an alumnus of the University of Delhi and the JNU. He worked for Indian Express and The Telegraph. He also worked with Ogilvy, McCann and JWT, before starting off as an independent professional servicing the communication needs of corporates. He teaches “Strategic Media Planning” at the Indian Institute of Mass Communication as an Adjunct faculty.

2 June 2018                           

Sankrant Sanu

Talk on
The English Medium Myth Dismantling Barriers to India’s Growth

Sankrant Sanu is an author, technologist and entrepreneur. He was a senior development manager at Microsoft where he co-founded Microsoft Sharepoint. He has been a serial entrepreneur since then, involved in startups in Seattle, Bay Area, Ottawa and Gurgaon. Some of his essays were published by Rupa in the book “Invading the Sacred.” He has written for Rediff, Swarajya, Entrepreneur Magazine, the Hindustan Times, Dainik Jagran, Amar Ujala and Manushi among others.
Sankrant is a Computer Science graduate of IIT Kanpur. Sankrant did graduate studies in Computer Science at The University of Texas at Austin. He teaches the Art of Living course. He holds six technology patents and blogs at sankrant.org

Hindol Sengupta

In the Age of Artificial Intelligence
A Talk on
Brahman and Free Will  Why Hindu arguments are 21st century ready

Hindol Sengupta is an Indian journalist and entrepreneur and the author of eight books. In 2017, he was selected as a Young Global Leader by the World Economic Forum. Among his recent books are Being Hindu, The Modern Monk: What Vivekananda Means To Us Today, and The Sacred Sword: The Legend of Guru Gobind Singh. Sengupta lives in Delhi and is currently Editor-at-Large at Fortune India. In 2015 his book on India's underbelly of entrepreneurship, ‘Recasting India’, was shortlisted for the Hayek Book Prize given by the Manhattan Institute.
